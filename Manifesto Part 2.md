# MANIFESTO PART 2: STOP OPTIMIZING WHAT ALREADY WORKS

* ***I do not need a perfect system. I need a working one.***
* If something already works well enough to let me create, think, and build, then it is not a problem to solve. It is a foundation to use.
* Constant improvement is not progress when it destroys continuity. ***A better system that breaks my momentum is worse than a stable system that is merely “good enough.”***
* ***I will not treat every improvement opportunity as an obligation.*** Not every “possible upgrade” deserves action. Most improvements are optional, not urgent.
* ***If I am actively working, creating, or in flow, I do not change my environment.*** I do not reinstall, migrate, switch, or rebuild systems during momentum. My work comes first. Optimization comes later, if at all.
* ***I allow myself to have “good enough” tools*** without turning them into projects. ***Tools are meant to disappear into the background*** so that I can focus on what I actually care about creating.
* Stability is not stagnation. ***Stability is what makes depth possible.***
* ***I choose continuity over novelty. I choose flow over control. I choose building over rebuilding.***
* ***Not everything needs to be improved.*** Some things only need to be used.
* If I notice the optimization urge popping up again, the goal isn’t to fight it or “win” against it—just to ***recognize it early and decide whether it actually serves what I do in general.***
* ***I will not be such a "technological perfectionist"*** - instead I shall strictly use standard tools and only upgrade when it meaningfully improves my current workflow without breaking continuity.
* I will therefore not worry so much about silly stuff such as ~"does this respect my privacy?"~ or ~"is this the most optimal way of doing things?"~. ***Instead I should and will ask myself "Does this serve my needs well enough?"***
